# YouTube Script Outline Builder

Convert a video topic and content research brief into a detailed, section-by-section script outline — with timing, hooks, transitions, and CTA placement mapped.

## Channel Context

**Niche:** B2B marketing, lead generation, AI automation, cold outreach, client acquisition, offer creation
**Audience:** Beginner to intermediate B2B agency owners, cold outreach practitioners, SaaS founders
**Tone:** Direct, practitioner, no guru fluff, systems-focused

## Video Structure Framework

### Standard Video Architecture (8–15 minutes)

```
[HOOK] — 0:00–0:45
[CONTEXT BRIDGE] — 0:45–1:30
[CORE CONTENT SECTIONS] — 1:30–[varies]
[RECAP] — Last 60–90 seconds
[CTA] — Final 30 seconds
```

Adjust timing per video length. Default target: 10–12 minutes for educational content.

---

### Section 1: HOOK (0:00–0:45)

Purpose: Stop the viewer from clicking away. Create immediate tension or curiosity.

Hook types (pick one per video):

**Type A — Pain Hook:**
Open with the exact painful situation the viewer is in.
"You've sent 500 cold emails this month. You got 3 replies. All of them said no."

**Type B — Contrarian Hook:**
Open with a statement that challenges what they believe.
"Personalization in cold email is mostly a waste of time. Here's what actually drives replies."

**Type C — Result Hook:**
Open with a specific outcome and immediately promise to show how.
"Using this exact 3-step sequence, I booked 11 meetings in 30 days with a list of 200 contacts."

**Type D — Question Hook:**
Open with the exact question they're Googling.
"Why does nobody reply to my cold emails even when I personalize them?"

Hook requirements:
- Must be spoken directly to camera or in VO over B-roll
- No intro music or logo animations before hook
- First sentence must contain the core tension of the video
- No "hey guys welcome back" before the hook

### Section 2: CONTEXT BRIDGE (0:45–1:30)

Purpose: Establish credibility and set up why this video exists.

Contains:
- 1–2 sentences on who you are and why you can talk about this (no long backstory)
- What the video will cover (explicit promise)
- Who this is for (audience qualifier — helps retention by filtering viewers)

Example:
"I run a B2B lead gen agency. I've sent over 50,000 cold emails. In this video I'm breaking down the 4 reasons most cold emails fail — and the exact fixes for each one. If you're an agency owner or doing outbound for a SaaS product, this is specifically for you."

### Section 3: CORE CONTENT SECTIONS (variable)

Structure depends on video type:

**Type 1 — Problem/Solution (3–5 problems with fixes)**
Best for: "Why X doesn't work" videos
Structure: Problem 1 → Root cause → Fix → Problem 2 → repeat

**Type 2 — Step-by-Step Tutorial (4–7 steps)**
Best for: How-to, system-building videos
Structure: Overview of steps → Step 1 in depth → Step 2 → repeat

**Type 3 — Framework Breakdown (3–4 components)**
Best for: Strategy or system explanation videos
Structure: Name the framework → Component 1 → Component 2 → How it works together

**Type 4 — Comparison/Versus**
Best for: Tool comparisons, strategy debates
Structure: Set up the comparison → Criterion 1 winner → Criterion 2 winner → Verdict

For each core section, the outline must specify:
- Section name
- Estimated duration
- Key point(s) to cover
- Supporting example or scenario
- Transition to next section

### Section 4: RECAP (last 60–90 seconds)

Purpose: Reinforce retention. YouTube's algorithm values watch time to the end.

Contains:
- Summary of the 3–5 most important points (1 sentence each)
- The single most important takeaway
- A bridge to the CTA

### Section 5: CTA (final 30 seconds)

One primary CTA per video. Do not stack multiple asks.

CTA options by goal:
- Subscribe: "If this was useful, subscribe — I post systems like this every [frequency]"
- Comment: "Drop your cold email open rate in the comments — I'll tell you what's wrong"
- Related video: "Watch this next — it covers [related problem]"
- Lead magnet: "I made a free [resource] that goes deeper on this — link in description"

## Outline Output Format

```
VIDEO OUTLINE
Topic: [title]
Type: [Problem/Solution / Tutorial / Framework / Comparison]
Target Duration: [X minutes]
Primary Keyword: [keyword]

---

HOOK (0:00–0:45) — [Hook Type]
Opening line: [exact first sentence]
Tension established: [what problem/curiosity is created]
Hook closes with: [transition into context bridge]

---

CONTEXT BRIDGE (0:45–1:30)
Credibility setup: [1–2 sentences]
Video promise: [what viewer will get]
Audience qualifier: [who this is for]

---

CORE SECTION 1: [Section Name] (1:30–X:XX)
Key point: [main argument]
Sub-points:
  - [sub-point 1]
  - [sub-point 2]
  - [sub-point 3]
Supporting example: [scenario or illustration]
Transition line: [how this connects to next section]

---

CORE SECTION 2: [Section Name] (X:XX–X:XX)
[repeat structure]

---

CORE SECTION 3: [Section Name] (X:XX–X:XX)
[repeat structure]

---

[Additional sections as needed]

---

RECAP (X:XX–X:XX)
Key takeaways:
1. [takeaway 1]
2. [takeaway 2]
3. [takeaway 3]
Single most important point: [1 sentence]

---

CTA (final 30 seconds)
Primary CTA: [type and exact wording]
Secondary element: [related video suggestion or none]

---

CHAPTERS (for description)
0:00 — [Hook/Intro title]
0:45 — [Context section title]
1:30 — [Core section 1 title]
[etc.]

---

OUTLINE NOTES
[Any flags, suggestions, or structural decisions the script writer should know]
```

## Quality Rules

Before outputting:
- ✓ Hook opens with tension — not a greeting or intro
- ✓ Every core section has a specific key point (not a vague theme)
- ✓ Supporting examples are included for at least 2 core sections
- ✓ Transitions are written out — not left to chance
- ✓ Only one CTA in the video
- ✓ Chapters are YouTube-ready (ready to paste into description)
- ✓ Target duration is realistic for the content depth
